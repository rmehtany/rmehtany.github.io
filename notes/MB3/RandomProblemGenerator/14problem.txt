Let \(P\) be chosen such that \(\triangle APB\) is an equalateral triangle. So, $$\angle CAP = \angle CAB - \angle PAB = 80^\circ - 60^\circ = 20^\circ = \angle BCD$$And, \(\overline{CD} = \overline{AB} = \overline{AP}\). Thus, by SAS, \(\triangle BDC\cong\triangle PCA\). Since \(\angle APC=\angle BPC\), $$\angle BDC = \angle APC = \frac{360^\circ-60^\circ}2=150^\circ$$So, \(\angle ADB = 180^\circ - \angle BDC = \color{red}{30^\circ}\).
