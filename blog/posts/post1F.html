Tamarind Rice (serves 3-5 people)
  • 1/4 cup
    Chana Dal (Optional)
  • 1/4 cup
    Urad Dal (Optional)
  • 2 tsp
    Mustard Seeds
  • 2
    Bay leaves (optional)
  • 10
    Neem leaves (optional)
  • 1/6 cup
    Dessicated Coconut Flakes
  • 1/6 cup
    Peanuts
  • 1 tbsp
    Cumin Powder (optional)
  • 1 tbsp
    Jaggery
  • 1.5 tbsp
    Tamarind Paste
  • 1 cup
    Basmati Rice (dry)
  • 1
    Red Chili (Optional)
Steam the basmati rice (or pressure cook it) until perfectly cooked (cooking time depends on the rice and device used to cook the rice).
Toast the urad dal, chana dal, mustard seeds, and peanuts on medium heat until fragrant. At this point, add the chili, bay leaves, and neem leaves to the pan.
On the side, mix tamarind paste, jaggery, and water in a bowl until a smooth liquid is formed.
Add the smooth liquid, cumin powder, and dessicated coconut to the pan, and mix till incorporated.
Turn down the heat, and mix in your rice. Cook until excess water is removed.

One of my favorite dishes growing up was tamarind rice. Sweet, savory, nutty rice was the perfect meal to accompany a hot summer day, and best of all, it's not too difficult to make!

Don't be intimidated by the ingredients! Most are either optional or have plenty of substitutes. It's up to you how authentic you want to be.

Recipe

1. Cook the rice (either steam or pressure cooker: we want the rice to be moist) until perfectly cooked. Unfortunately, this is very dependent on the cooking device used and the type of basmati rice, so look for instructions for your device of choice. If you can't find Basmati rice at your local Indian grocery store, any long grain white rice should do.

2. On a pan on medium heat, toast the urad dal, chana dal, mustard seeds, and peanuts until fragrant. Almost any dry lentils should be a good substitutes for the dals, but cooking time might differ.

3. At this point, add the chili, bay leaves, and neem leaves. Neem leaves have an aroma similar to that of basil/holy basil, so that's a decent substitute.

4. On the side, mix tamarind paste, jaggery, and water in a bowl till smooth. Jaggery might be hard to find if you don't have access to a local Indian grocery, but brown sugar is a decent substitute.

5. Add the mixture, cumin powder, and dessicated coconut to the pan and incorporate. Fresh shaved coconut, coconut milk, or other coconut products could be a substitute for dessicated coconut. Garam Masala, dried oregano, or dried thyme could substitute for cumin powder.

6. Turn down the heat, mix in the rice, and cook on low heat until excess water is removed.
