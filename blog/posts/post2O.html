While deriving my proof for the first beautiful geometric diagram, I found a rather beautiful relation involving the regular polygons with an even number of sides.

For the rest of the post, assume that all polygons are regular with side length \(1\).

Products of partitions of the main diagonal

By our analysis from the previous post, we know that the lengths of the partitions for a polygon with \(2m\) sides are $$\sin\frac\pi{2m},\;\sin\frac{3\pi}{2m},\ldots,\;\sin\frac{(2m-1)\pi}{2m}$$So more simply put, we want to show the follow equality holds:$$\prod\limits_{k=1}^m\sin\left(\frac{(2k-1)\pi}{2m}\right)=2^{1-m}$$

To show this, we first show that if we let \(F(m)=\prod\limits_{k=1}^{m-1}\sin\frac{k\pi}m\), then \(F(m)=m\cdot2^{1-m}\). This is because $$\prod\limits_{k=1}^{m-1}\sin\frac{k\pi}m=(2i)^{1-n}\prod\limits_{k=1}^{m-1}\left(\exp\left(\frac{ki\pi}m\right)-\exp\left(-\frac{ki\pi}m\right)\right)$$by the relation \(\sin x=(2i)^{-1}(\exp(ix)-\exp(-ix))\)$$=(2i)^{1-n}\exp\left(-\frac{i(n-1)n}2\right)\prod\limits_{k=1}^{m-1}\left(\exp\left(\frac{2ki\pi}m\right)-1\right)$$by pulling out a \(\exp\left(\frac{2ki\pi}m\right)\) term from the multiplicands$$=(-2)^{1-n}\prod\limits_{k=1}^{m-1}\left(\exp\left(\frac{2i\pi}m\right)^k-1\right)$$By evaluating the term outside the product$$=2^{1-n}\prod\limits_{k=1}^{m-1}\left(1-\exp\left(\frac{2i\pi}m\right)^k\right)$$\(\zeta=\exp\left(\frac{2i\pi}m\right)\) is the primitive \(m\)th root of unity, so the \(m\) roots of unity are generated by the first \(m\) powers of \(\zeta\). In particular, we have for any complex \(x\), $$(x-1)\cdot\sum\limits_{k=0}^{m-1}x^k=x^m-1=(x-1)\cdot\prod\limits_{k=1}^{m-1}(1-\zeta^k)$$Thus, letting \(x=1\) in the equation above gives us$$2^{1-n}\prod\limits_{k=1}^{m-1}\left(1-\zeta^k\right)=2^{1-n}\cdot\sum\limits_{k=0}^{m-1}1^k=m\cdot2^{1-n}$$

Now that we have a closed form for \(F(m)\), observe that $$\prod\limits_{k=1}^m\sin\left(\frac{(2k-1)\pi}{2m}\right)=\frac{\prod\limits_{r=1}^{2m-1}\sin\left(\frac{r\pi}{2m}\right)}{\prod\limits_{s=1}^{m-1}\sin\left(\frac{2s\pi}{2m}\right)}=\frac{\prod\limits_{r=1}^{2m-1}\sin\left(\frac{r\pi}{2m}\right)}{\prod\limits_{s=1}^{m-1}\sin\left(\frac{s\pi}m\right)}=\frac{F(2m)}{F(m)}$$And the last fraction equals \(2^{1-m}\) as desired.

Exercise

We derived a relation on the product of partitions of the main diagonal for even side length regular polygons. How about odd side length polygons? (Hint: You get a nice formula if the side length is \(2\)).
