Who betrayed Duke Leto?

Duke Leto Atreides knows for a fact that there are not one, but two traitors within his royal household. The suspects are Lady Jessica, Dr. Wellington Yueh, Gurney Halleck and Duncan Idaho. Leto’s advisor, Thufir Hawat, will assist him in questioning the four suspects. Anyone who is a traitor will tell a lie, while anyone who is not a traitor will tell the truth.

Upon interrogation, Jessica says that she is not the traitor, while Wellington similarly says that he is not the traitor. Gurney says that either Jessica or Wellington is a traitor. Finally, Duncan says that Jessica or Gurney is a traitor.

After playing back the interrogations in his mind, Thufir is ready to report the name of one of the traitors to the duke. Whose name does he report?

There are two cases to consider.

Case 1: Idaho is not a traitor: In this case, if Halleck is a traitor, then none of Jessica, Yueh, or Idaho can be a traitor. However, there are two traitors, so this isn't possible. Thus, Halleck must be good, and thus the traitors are Jessica and Yueh.

Case 2: Idaho is a traitor: In this case, neither Jessica nor Halleck are traitors, so Idaho and Yueh are the traitors.

In both cases, Yueh is a traitor, so we can be confident in announcing his name as our first traitor.

Riddler Classic

Suppose you have an equilateral triangle. You pick three random points, one along each of its three edges, uniformly along the length of each edge — that is, each point along each edge has the same probability of being selected.

With those three randomly selected points, you can form a new triangle inside the original one. What is the probability that the center of the larger triangle also lies inside the smaller one?

Let's place our equilateral triangle on the coordinate plane. It will have vertices \((0,0)\), \((1,0)\), \(\left(\frac12,\frac{\sqrt3}2\right)\). Now, I fix a point \((t,0)\) on the bottom side of the triangle. Observe that the problem is symmetric: the probability of the center being in the triangle with \((t,0)\) as one of the coordinates is the same as that of being in the center with \((1-t,0)\) as one of the coordinates.

So, for simplicity, we'll let \(t\in(0,0.5)\), and multiply our end result by \(2\) to account for this. Notice that regardless of where the point on the left face of the triangle is, as long as the point on the right face of the triangle is in the black section of the animation above, the triangle contains the center.

So how do we calculate this? Well, the purple line is the line that goes through the points \((t,0)\) and \(\left(\frac12,\frac1{2\sqrt3}\right)\), which is given by $$y=\frac{x-t}{\sqrt3(1-2t)}$$The green line (covered by the black segment) is the line through the points \((1,0)\) and \(\left(\frac12,\frac{\sqrt3}2\right)\), which is $$y=-\sqrt3x+\sqrt3$$The intersection of these two points is at $$\left(\frac{5t-3}{6t-4},\frac{\sqrt3(t-1)}{6t-4}\right)$$Thus, the distance between \((1,0)\) and this point, which tells us the probability of the chosen triangle containing the center if the first chosen point is \((t,0)\), is $$\sqrt{\left(\frac{t-1}{6t-4}\right)^2+\left(\sqrt3\frac{t-1}{6t-4}\right)^2}=2\left\vert\frac{t-1}{6t-4}\right\vert$$So, our answer is $$4\int\limits_0^{0.5}\left\vert\frac{t-1}{6t-4}\right\vert\;dt\sim0.4621$$So, the probability is approximately \(\color{red}{46.21\%}\).
